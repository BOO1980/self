What is social anxiety

  • Social anxiety is a term that describes the fear, nervousness and apprehension most people at times experience in their relationships with other people
  • Social anxiety strikes people when they think they might do something that will be humiliating or embarrasing
  • Social anxiety make you think other people are judging you and doing so in a negative way because of something you said or did
  • The fear that you will do something humiliating or embarrassing is inhibiting
  • Socially anxious people tend to assume that their interactions with others will be painfully revealing
  • People will notice their weaknesses or awkwardness
  • That they can be dismissed or ignored, critisied or rejected for not behaving more acceptably
  • Seeing things this way make it hard to interact naturally with people, and difficult to talk, listen or make friends
  • Social anxiety often leads to isolation and loneliness
  • For many people one of the sadnesses of suffering from this problem is that it prevents them becoming intimate with other people
  • Feeling at ease in company is hard for them and it makes them so anxious that good qualities of the personality are hidden from view
  • anxiety interferes with their expression and the abilkity to show them may have gone rusty from lack of use
  • Social anxious people may have lost belief in their likeable qaulities together with their self confidence
  • Overcoming social anxiety allows you to enjoy rather than fear being yourself
  • Overcoming SA allow you to discover or to resdiscover yourself
  • Overcoming SA allows you to trust rather than doubt yourself

  • A definition: The individiual fears that he or she will act in a way that will be embarrasing and humiliating
  • A definition: Exposed to the feared social situations almost invariably provokes social anxiety
  • A definition: The person recognises that the fear is unreasonable or excessive
