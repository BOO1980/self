Social Anxiety Overview

  • In order to overcome a problem it is important to understand what is keeping it going.

What is Social anxiety?

  • Socail Anxiety is a shorthand term that describes the fear, nervousness and apprehension most people at times experience in their relationships with other people
  • Some people who suffer from social anxiety would say they were shy and may have been shy all of their lives
  • Social anxiety strikes people when they think they might do something that will be humilating or embarrassing
  • Social anxiety makes people think they are judging you and doing so in a negative way because of something you said or did.
  • The fear that you will do something humiliating or embarrassing is inhibiting
  • It will make your self-conscious aware of the possibility that you might indeed so such a thing
  • Who would want to get into a converation if they thought that doing so would only reveal their clumsiness or inadequacy
  • Socially anxious people tend to assume their interactions with others will be painfully revealing that others will notice their weaknesses, that they will be dismissed, ignored, criticised or rejected for not behaving more acceptably
  • Seeing things this way makes it hard to interact naturally with people and difficult to talk, listen or make friends.
  • Often it leads to isolation and loneliness, and for so many people one of the sadnesses of suffering from thsi problem is that it prevents them from being intimate with other people, or finding a partner to share their lives
  • Socially anxious people usually feel friendly towards others and have their fair share of the positive characteristics that other people appreciate
  • They may have a sense of fun, be engergetic and generous, kind and understanding, serious, amusing, quiet or lively and spontaneously behave in these ways if they feel at ease.
  • Feeling at ease in company is hard for them, and makes them so anxious, that these qualities are hidden from view
  • Soically anxious people may have lost belief in their likeable qualities together with their self-confidence
  • One of the rewards of learning to overcome social anxiety is that it enables you to express aspects of yourself that may previously have been stifled
  • It allows you to enjoy, rather than to fear being yourself
  • Overcoming anxiety allows you to rediscover or even discover yourself
  • As you come to realise that no-one's social behaviour is perfect and everyone makes mistakes
  • Mistakes are fine - they are only part of being human so there is no needs to let them under-mine you.

Defining the problem

  • Social anxiety is normal and everybody geels it sometimes so everyone knows to some degree what it is like
  • Seeking a total cure would be attempting the impossible
  • Becoming less self-conscious can make a big difference and reducing pain and distress which helps you live the life you wish to live
  • The main four criteria
    1. A persistent fear of one or more social or performance situations in which the person is exposed to unfamiliar people or possible scrutiny by others
    2. The individual fears that he or she will act in a way that will be embarrassing and humilating [note: people with social phobia may not actually do anythinh humilating or embarrassing - they only fear that they will
    3. Exposure to the feared social situations almost invariably provokes anxiety
    4. The precise things that someone with Social Anxiety Disorder fears differ from person to person, having to do these things are always difficult for them
    5. The person with SAD recognises that the fear is unreasonable or excessive
    6. You know that the things that make you anxious are not really dangerous and they may hardly bother other people at all
    7. SAD can make you feel unconfident, indaequate or inferior as well as anxious
    8. The feared situations are avoided or else endured with intense anxiety and distress
    9. It is only natural then it makes sense in terms of self-protection to avoid or escape from frightening protection
    10. people with SAD do not want to be lonely and isolated and they cannot control the sources of their fear
    11. People with SAD want to work, make friends and feel that they belong just as much as others, so they may endure difficult situations despite the distress that they feel, and focus on keeping the risks or threats as small as possible, the intensity of the anxiety they feel makes the stratergy seem only sensible

    An example of a typical chain reaction

    • You feel anxious and ill at ease when interacting with others, or being observed by them
    • You fear doing something humilating or embarassing which you think that others will notice and judge you negatively
    • When you feel anxious your attention focuses inward on to yourself, makin gyou consious and super aware of your feelings and actions
    • This makes it impossible to give others your full attention, so you can go on thinking that social situations will be uncomforable, threatening or downright dangerous
    • You try to protect yourself by avoiding difficult things and by using safety behaviours
    • Eventually you start to anticipate being left out, or critised or rejected. You start to assume that interactions will not go well for you
    • The anxiety makes it hard to relax and be yourself, after a time this undermines your confidence and shakes your self esteem
